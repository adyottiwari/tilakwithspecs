Ashtayam Seva — The Eight Daily Darshans of Shri Thakur Ji

अष्टयाम सेवा में राग और भाव विधान — पुष्टिमार्गीय परम्परा

The Foundation

What is Haveli Sangeet?

Haveli Sangeet is the devotional music tradition of Pushtimarg, established by Gosaiji Shri Vitthalnathji (son of Shri Vallabhacharyaji) in the 16th century. It is one of the oldest and most preserved forms of Indian classical music, rooted in the ancient Drupad-Dhamar tradition.

The word "Haveli" refers to the grand mansion-temples where Shri Thakur Ji resides. "Sangeet" means music. So Haveli Sangeet is literally — the music of the divine mansion.

हवेली संगीत केवल गायन नहीं है — यह प्रभु श्री ठाकुर जी की सेवा का एक दिव्य अंग है। जो सुर आलापा जाता है, वह प्रभु के चरणों में अर्पित होता है।

The Three Pillars of Seva

Shri Vitthalnathji expanded worship into three inseparable pillars: Raag (music), Bhog (food offerings), and Shringar (adornment). Each supports the other — Raag creates the bhav, Shringar beautifies the swaroop, Bhog nourishes Prabhu.

The Drupad Tradition

Haveli Sangeet preserves Drupad — the oldest form of Indian classical music. While Drupad nearly vanished in the 18th century with the rise of Khayaal, the Pushtimarg havelis kept it alive for over 500 years. Instruments: Pakhawaj, Sarangi, Tanpura, Veena, Flute, Jhanz.

The Eight Poet-Devotees

The Ashta Chaap

To formalize Haveli Sangeet, Shri Vitthalnathji appointed Ashtachhap — eight poet-devotees who composed kirtans in specific raags for the Ashtayam Seva. Their compositions remain the living heart of Haveli Sangeet today.

Sevaks of Shri Mahaprabhuji:

Shri Surdas — 1,25,000 padas (5,000 survive)

Shri Kumbhandas — First to sing kirtan before Shri Thakur Ji

Shri Paramanandas — 2,000 padas

Shri Krishnadas — 1,200 padas

Shri Vishnudas Chippa — later replaced by Shri Nanddas

Sevaks of Shri Vitthalnathji:

Shri Govindswami — 400 padas

Shri Chaturbhujdas — 200 padas

Shri Chittaswami — 200 padas

Shri Nanddas — 300 padas (cousin of Tulsidas)

अष्टछाप मण्डल — सन् १६०२ में पूर्ण हुआ।

Distinctive Features

What Makes Haveli Sangeet Unique?

🎵 Not for Entertainment

Sung exclusively for Shri Thakur Ji's pleasure. The kirtankaar faces the deity — not the audience. This is seva, not performance.

⏳ The Prahar System

Each raag belongs to a specific time of day. Singing the wrong raag at the wrong time disturbs the natural cosmic energy. The day has its own raga-grammar.

🌿 500 Years Unbroken

While Drupad nearly vanished from Indian classical music, Pushtimarg havelis preserved it without interruption for five centuries.

Darshan by Darshan

Raags, Bhaav & Sacred Kirtans

01 · First Darshan

Mangala Darshan

मंगला — प्रभु श्री ठाकुर जी का प्रथम जगना

~4:30 – 6:00 AM

The day begins in the deepest stillness before dawn. Shri Thakur Ji is awakened with the blowing of the conch shell and the gentle unfolding of morning raags. The conch calls first — then the pakhawaj, tanpura, and flute. The bhav is of the most tender waking: a mother calling her beloved child from sleep.

Primary Raags
Raag Bhairav Raag Lalit (Shuddh Dhaivat) Raag Bibhas
Bhaav of Mangala Seva
जगना — Awakening वात्सल्य — Motherly Love प्रभात — Dawn Solemnity अनुराग — Deep Devotion खंडिता — Spurned Longing दाहीमंथन — Churning of Curd
Raag Bhairav The quintessential dawn raag — solemn, devotional, deeply peaceful. Komal Rishabh and Dhaivat give it a quality of total surrender at Prabhu's feet.
Raag Lalit Sung just before sunrise. Its rare double-Madhyam structure creates a tender, incomplete longing — as if the soul is calling out to Prabhu to open His eyes.
Kirtan — Mangala Pada · Vatsalya Bhav

जागिये गोपाल लाल जननी बलि जाई

उठो तात भयो प्रात रजनी को तिमिर घट्यो,
आये सब ग्वालबाल मोहना कन्हाई ॥

उठो मेरे आनंद कंद, चंद किरण मंद भई,
प्रकट्यो आकाश भानु कमलन सुखदाई।
संगी सब चरात धेनु, तुम बिना न बाजी वेणु,
उठो लाल तजो सेज सुंदर वरराई ॥
Kirtan — Mangala Pada · Shri Surdas Ji

जागिये ब्रजराज कुंवर कमल कोश फूले

कुमुदिनी जिय सकुच रही, भृंगलता झूले ॥
तमचर खग रोर करत, बोलत बन मांहि,
रांभत गऊ मधुर नाद, बच्छन हित धाई ॥
'सूर' श्री गोपाल उठे आनन्द मंगलकारी ॥
Historical Note

The very first kirtan of Pushtimarg was sung by Shri Kumbhandasji at Mangala in Bilaval Raag and Dhammar Taal — with the bhav of खंडिता (the spurned beloved). This kirtan, "सांझ के सांचे बोल तिहारे," gave birth to the entire kirtan tradition of Pushtimarg.

02 · Second Darshan

Shringar Darshan

श्रृंगार — वस्त्र, आभूषण, अर्पण की सेवा

~7:00 – 8:00 AM

After being gently bathed, Shri Thakur Ji is adorned with seasonal clothes and jewellery — pearls in summer, precious stones in winter, gold in spring. The kirtankaar sings as each ornament is lovingly offered. The raags here are joyful and radiant, celebrating the beauty of the adorned beloved.

Primary Raags
Raag Sorath Raag Dhanashree Raag Hindol / Basant Raag Malhar
Bhaav of Shringar Seva
सौंदर्य — Divine Beauty हर्ष — Joyful Celebration स्नेह — Affectionate Love उत्सव — Festivity दासी भाव — Handmaiden's Love ऋतु भाव — Seasonal Devotion
Kirtan — Shringar Pada

आओ गोपाल श्रृंगार बनाऊ

विविध सुगंधन करो उबटनो, पाछे उष्णजल ले जू न्हवावू ॥
अंग अंगोछ गुहूं तेरी बेनी, फूलन रची रची भाल बनाऊ।
सुरंग पाग जरतारी तोरा, रत्नखचित शिरपेच बंधाऊ ॥
गजमोतींन के हार मनोहर, वनमाला ले उर पहेराऊ।
ले दर्पण देखो मेरे प्यारे, निरख निरख उर नयन सिराऊ ॥
Kirtan — Shringar Pada · Shri Nanddas Ji

नंद भवन को भूषण माई

यशुदा को लाल, वीर हलधर को, राधारमण सदा सुखदाई ॥
इंद्र को इंद्र, देव देवन को, ब्रह्म को ब्रह्म, महा बलदाई।
'नंददास' को जीवन गिरिधर, गोकुल मंडन कुंवर कन्हाई ॥
Seasonal Shringar Note

ऋतु के अनुसार राग और श्रृंगार दोनों बदलते हैं। In summer — pearls and white clothes. In monsoon — Malhar and green. In winter — precious stones and red. In spring — Hindol-Basant and yellow. This is one of the most beautiful expressions of Pushtimarg's attentiveness to Prabhu's comfort.

03 · Third Darshan

Gwal Darshan

ग्वाल — माखन चोरी, वात्सल्य, गोचरण की झाँकी

~9:00 – 10:00 AM

Shri Thakur Ji sets off with the cowherd boys into the forests of Vraj. The kirtans burst open with the playfulness of childhood — butter-stealing, mischief, the laughter of Nandnandan. The raags are bright, pastoral, and full of energy — capturing the freshness of a Vraj morning in full sunlight.

Primary Raags
Raag Dhanashri Raag Gaud Sarang Raag Vrundavani Sarang Raag Gauri / Kanharo
Bhaav of Gwal Seva
वात्सल्य — Parental Love बाल लीला — Childhood Play माखन चोरी — Butter Theft सखा भाव — Friendship उत्साह — Energetic Joy
Kirtan — Gwal Pada · Shri Paramanandas Ji

गोपाल माई खेलत है चकडोरी

लरका पांच सात संग लीने, निपट सांकरी खोरी ॥
चढ घर होंरी झरोखा चितयो, सखी लियो मन चोरी।
बांए हाथ वलैया लीनी, अपनो अंचर छोरी ॥
चारों नयन मिले जब सन्मुख, रसिक हँसे मुख मोर।
परमानंद दास रतिनागर, चित में लई रति जोर ॥
Vrundavani Sarang — The Forest Raag

Raag Vrundavani Sarang carries the soul of Vrindavan's forests — वृन्दावन के कुञ्जों की छाया। Its bright, mid-morning energy is inseparable from the image of Prabhu playing among the cowherd boys. This raag belongs exclusively to this hour, this lila.

04 · Fourth Darshan

Rajbhog Darshan

राजभोग — दोपहर का मुख्य एवं भव्य भोग

~11:00 AM – 12 PM

The grandest bhog of the day — an elaborate offering worthy of a reigning monarch. Shri Thakur Ji returns from the forest and is served the midday meal with full royal honours. The raags here are majestic and full-bodied, reflecting the peak energy of noon. After Rajbhog, Prabhu rests.

Primary Raags
Raag Gaud Sarang Raag Vrundavani Sarang Raag Shuddh Sarang Raag Noor Sarang Raag Bilawal Raag Hindol Vasant
Bhaav of Rajbhog Seva
प्रेम — Deep Love राजभाव — Royal Majesty तृप्ति — Satisfaction दासभाव — Servant's Offering समर्पण — Complete Surrender मधुर — Sweetness
Kirtan — Rajbhog Pada · Shri Paramanandas Ji

आज दधि मीठो मदन गोपाल

भावत मोहि तिहारो झूंठो चंचल नैन बिसाल ॥
आने पात बनाये दोना दिये सबनको बांट।
जिन नहिं पायो सुनो रे भैया मेरी अंगुरिन चाट ॥
बहुत दिनन हम रहे कुमुदवन कृष्ण तिहारे साथ।
परमानंद प्रभु हम सब जानत तुम त्रिभुवन के भूप ॥
Kirtan — Rajbhog Pada · Shri Govindswami Ji

ऐसी प्रीति कहूँ नहि देखी

जसुमति-सुत वल्लभ-सुत जैसी सेस सहस मुख जात न लेखी ॥
आग्यां माँगि चलत गोकुल को छिनु छिनु झाँकि झरोखन पेखी।
'गोविंद' प्रभु गोवद्धन पै माँगत बिछुरो पल जिन अर्ध निमेखी ॥
05 · Fifth Darshan

Utthapan Darshan

उत्थापन — दोपहर के विश्राम के उपरांत जगना

~3:00 – 4:00 PM

Prabhu is awakened from the afternoon rest. Unlike Mangala's solemn dawn-breaking, Utthapan has a softer, more intimate quality — there is affectionate insistence, the playful urgency of a mother calling her child back from play. The scene returns to gocharan in the afternoon light.

Primary Raags
Raag Shuddh Sarang / Vrundavani Sarang Raag Nat Raag Gauri Raag Bhimpalasi
Bhaav of Utthapan Seva
प्रेमाकुलता — Loving Urgency विरह — Gentle Longing आकुलता — Restless Waiting वात्सल्य — Parental Tenderness सांध्य-भाव — Afternoon Peace
Kirtan — Utthapan Pada

आओ मेरे गोविंद गोकुल के चंदा

भई बडी वार खेलत यमुना तट वदन दिखाय देउ आनंदा ॥
गाय आवन की भई है विरियां दिनमणि किरण होत अति मंदा।
आये मेरे तात मात छतियां लाग गोविंद प्रभु व्रज-जन सुखकंदा ॥
Kirtan — Utthapan Pada · Govind Bala

मोहन नयन नहुंते नहिं टरत

विन देखे तालावेली सी लागत देखत मन जो हरत ॥
अशन वसन सेन न की सुध आवे अब कछू न करत।
गोविंद बल इम कहत पियारी तृसिख देरी के सेक आवे भरत ॥
Bhimpalasi — The Transitional Raag

Raag Bhimpalasi bridges afternoon and evening — its komal Gandhar and Nishad create a contemplative, slightly longing quality. यह राग भावुकता और शांति का अनूठा संगम है — the perfect raag for the softened light of late afternoon when Prabhu is gently roused.

06 · Sixth Darshan

Bhog Darshan & Aarti

भोग आरती — गोपी-भाव, मुरली, युगल-किशोर आरती

~4:30 – 5:30 PM

An evening bhog is offered with deep intimacy. Kirtans here are enriched with Gopidasha (the perspective of the gopis), Murali bhav (the call of the flute), and the love of Prabhu's devotees as He moves through the evening. The aarti of Yugal Kishor is performed — and the raags deepen with the falling light.

Primary Raags
Raag Kanhra (Kanharo) Raag Yaman Raag Gaud Sarang Raag Bhimpalasi
Bhaav of Bhog Seva
मुरली भाव — Flute's Call गोपी दशा — Gopi's Longing प्रेम — Divine Love गोपा भाव — Cowherd's Joy आरती भाव — Devotional Aarti कृष्ण रूप — Form Contemplation
Kirtan — Bhog Pada · Shri Chittaswami Ji

आगे गाय पाछें गाय इत गाय उत गाय

गोविंद को गायन में बस बोइ भावे।
गायन के संग धावें, गायन में सचु पावें,
गायन की खुर रज अंग लपटावे ॥
गायन सो ब्रज छायो, बैकुंठ बिसरायो,
'छीतस्वामी' गिरिधारी, विट्ठलेश वपुधारी,
ग्वारिया को भेष धरें गायन में आवे ॥
Kirtan — Aarti Pada · Shri Paramanandas Ji

आरती युगल किशोर की कीजे

तन मन धन न्योछावर दीजे ॥
गौर श्याम मुख निरखत जीजे, प्रेम-स्वरूप नयन भर पीजे ॥
रवि शशि कोटि वदन की शोभा, ताहि देखत मेरो मन लोभा ॥
नंद-नंदन वृषभान-किशोरी, परमानंद प्रभु अविचल जोरी ॥
07 · Seventh Darshan

Sandhya Aarti

संध्या आरती — माँ यशोदा द्वारा आरती, गोधूलि की झाँकी

~6:30 – 7:00 PM

As the cows return home and the golden dust of dusk rises on the Vraj paths, mother Yashoda performs the aarti of Shri Thakur Ji. This is Godohan — the milking of cows. The raags are suffused with viraha (longing) and the warmth of homecoming. This is perhaps the most emotionally vivid and moving darshan of the day.

Primary Raags
Raag Sorath Raag Yaman Raag Hamir Raag Purvi
Bhaav of Sandhya Aarti
विरह — Loving Separation गोधूलि — Dusty Dusk माधुर्य — Sweet Longing यशोदा भाव — Mother's Aarti प्रतीक्षा — Anticipation गोपिका प्रेम — Gopi Love
Raag Purvi Deeply twilight — its flat Rishabh and Dhaivat with sharp Madhyam create a bittersweet, aching longing unique to the dusk hour. One of the most evocative raags in the tradition.
Raag Hamir Majestic and full-hearted. As Prabhu returns home in the evening light, Hamir reflects the grandeur and celebration of His homecoming to Yashoda's arms.
The Bhav of Sandhya — गोधूलि

गोधूलि की वेला में जब गाएँ घर लौटती हैं, तब माँ यशोदा आरती उतारती हैं। The dust of the cows' hooves in the golden evening — this is the eternal, irreplaceable image of Sandhya in Pushtimarg. Every evening aarti in every haveli, everywhere in the world, recreates this exact moment in Vraj.

08 · Eighth Darshan

Shayan Darshan

शयन — निकुञ्ज में रात्रि विश्राम, संयोग श्रृंगार

~8:30 – 9:30 PM

The day's final darshan — dinner is offered, and Shri Thakur Ji is prepared for rest. Kirtans here enter the deepest and most intimate register of the entire tradition — describing the Nikunj bhavana (the secret bower of Vrindavan) and sanyog shringar. These raags are deep, nocturnal, slow — a lullaby for Prabhu Himself.

Primary Raags
Raag Bihag Raag Kanharo (Kanra) Raag Yaman Raag Hamir Raag Adana Raag Nayaki
Bhaav of Shayan Seva
निकुञ्ज भाव — Bower's Intimacy संयोग — Divine Union शांति — Utter Peace नायिका भाव — Beloved's Longing मधुर विरह — Sweet Separation लोरी — Divine Lullaby
Raag Bihag The quintessential late-night raag of devotion — rich, romantic, and suffused with divine love. Its smooth movement makes it the most fitting companion for Prabhu's rest.
Raag Darbari Grand, slow, and immensely deep. Tansen is said to have composed it for Akbar's court at night. In the haveli, it carries the weight of the night's silence and surrender.
Raag Adana Late-night, intense, and full of yearning. Used in Shayan to express the soul's deep longing for Prabhu — the devotee as lover, crying out in the night.
Raag Nayaki A rare and intimate raag — नायकी भाव — expressing the devotee as the beloved of Prabhu, in the madhura bhakti tradition. Sung only at this final hour.
Kirtan — Shayan Pada · Shri Vitthalnathji

सखियन रुचि रुचि सेज बनाई

रंग महल में पेरे हें परदा धरी अंगीठी सुखदाई ॥
शीत समें ग्रीष्म ऋतु कीनी अति सुंदर वरराईं।
श्री विट्ठल गिरिधारी कृपानिधि पोढे ओढ रजाई ॥
Shayan — The Deepest Bhav

The kirtans of Shayan describe निकुञ्ज — वृंदावन का वह गुप्त कुञ्ज जहाँ प्रभु विराजते हैं। These are not sung for any audience. They are whispered in Prabhu's presence as He is laid to rest. This is the most intimate and sacred register of all of Pushtimarg seva.

Practical Guidance

How to Perform Raag Seva

Raag Seva is not performance. It is seva.

When you sing during darshan, you are serving Shri Thakur Ji by creating the right atmosphere for His lila. You are entering the lila yourself — not as a spectator but as a participant. You are aligning with cosmic time — matching the energy of the universe at that exact moment.

राग सेवा में गायक श्रोताओं की ओर नहीं — प्रभु की ओर मुख करके गाता है। क्योंकि यह प्रदर्शन नहीं, यह प्रभु को प्रसन्न करने की सेवा है।

Before You Begin

Purify yourself — bathe, wear clean clothes.

Pranaam to your Guru and to Shri Thakur Ji — never sing without seeking blessings first.

Pranaam to the Raag itself — the raag is divine, worthy of reverence.

Know the time — confirm you are singing the correct raag for the prahar.

After You Finish

Pranaam again — to Shri Thakur Ji, to the raag, and to your Guru.

Sit in silence briefly — let the bhav settle before moving.

जब तक भाव मन में है, उसे बाहर न जाने दें। वह क्षण भी सेवा है।

✅ Do — अवश्य करें

Learn the prahar system — which raag goes at which time

Practice bhav — emotion matters more than technical mastery

Face the deity, not the audience — always

Let the raag breathe — don't rush the alaap

Listen to authentic Haveli Sangeet and learn from a Guru

Regional Traditions

Three Branches of Haveli Sangeet

🏯 Nathdwara · Kankroli

The most formal tradition — preserved in the main Shreenathji haveli in Rajasthan. The seat of the Tilakayat, a direct descendant of Shri Gusaiji.

🌊 Gokul · Mathura · Vrindavan

Where it all began. The origin of Haveli Sangeet — where Shri Kumbhandasji first sang before Shri Thakur Ji at Govardhan.

🪷 Gujarat Tradition

Ahmedabad, Vadodara, Surat, Jamnagar — when svaroops migrated during Aurangzeb's persecution. Distinct regional style, same sacred core.

Why Haveli Sangeet Matters

Haveli Sangeet is one of the oldest living music traditions in the world. When you sing raag seva — you are not just singing. You are continuing a 500-year-old lineage. You are not just performing — you are participating in the eternal lila of Shri Thakur Ji. You are not just making sound — you are offering your voice as seva.
